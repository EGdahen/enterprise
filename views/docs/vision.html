The "why" is as important as the "what"

Natural &
Intuitive

Make Complex things comprehensible.

Complex problems and tasks require complex interfaces. By using consistent patterns, familiar affordances, and good visual organization, even a complex interface can be understandable.

Guide users.

Users are empowered when they know where they are and what is happening. Visual cues like breadcrumbs say "you are here". Visual feedback like state changes say "you just did something".

Make help unnecessary.

The most common interactions should be intuitive. From day one, users should be able to do most of their work without manuals. Of course, if they do need help, answers should be easy to find and immediately relevant.

Make it easy to recover.

Everybody should have a do-over (or at least an undo). Make it easy for users to recover if they make a mistake, forget a password, or get cut off in the middle of their work.

Keep simple things simple.

Simple tasks should be easy to accomplish. Wherever possible, give users just enough to do the job quickly and correctly.
