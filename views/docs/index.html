UX & Research

Consider the shower faucet. Once upon a time, shower faucets were simple contraptions. There was a old-water knob and there was a hot-water knob. You would turn each knob to adjust the temperature;you didn’t have to figure out how to use it. It was obvious. Then, in the Sixties or Seventies, some engineer came along and decided that maybe you could combine the cold and hot water faucets into one faucet. Suddenly figuring out how to work the temperature and water pressure became infinitely more complicated. There arrived on the market a plethora of new faucets, each operating differently.
